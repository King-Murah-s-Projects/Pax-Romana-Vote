# Pax Romana - KNUST Election System Overview

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose of the Document
This document provides a comprehensive overview of the Pax Romana - KNUST Election System, a web-based application designed to facilitate a paperless nomination and voting process for student elections. It outlines the system's key features, user roles, and benefits, serving as a reference for stakeholders, including students, administrators, and developers.

### 1.2 Purpose of the System
The Pax Romana - KNUST Election System aims to streamline the student election process by transitioning from traditional paper-based methods to a secure, efficient, and accessible online platform. By leveraging the existing Pax Web App, the system enhances transparency, reduces administrative overhead, and ensures a fair electoral process for all participants.

---

## 2. System Overview

The Pax Romana - KNUST Election System is an extension of the existing Pax Web App, specifically tailored to manage the nomination, verification, voting, and result-sharing phases of the student election process. The system is designed to be user-friendly, secure, and compliant with the organization's electoral rules and university data protection policies.

---

## 3. Key Features

The system offers the following key features to support a seamless election process:

### 3.1 Online Nomination Form Submission
- Aspirants can complete and submit nomination forms directly through the Pax Web App.
- The form captures essential details, including personal information, subgroup affiliations, educational background, leadership experience, skills, and vision for the office.
- Form submissions are validated for completeness and accuracy before acceptance.

### 3.2 Nominator and Guarantor Verification
- Upon nomination form submission, nominators and guarantors receive automated email notifications with unique links to verify their support.
- Nominators and guarantors must confirm their endorsement and provide reasons for their support via the provided link.
- The system records these responses, and nominations are only validated once all required verifications are received.

### 3.3 Secure Online Voting
- On the election day, registered users can cast their votes through the Pax Web App.
- Voting is restricted to users who have signed up before the election day, with new sign-ups disabled to prevent unauthorized voting.
- Each voter can cast one vote per position, and the system ensures that votes are recorded securely and anonymously.

### 3.4 Result Compilation and Verification
- After voting concludes, the system automatically compiles the results and generates detailed reports.
- The electoral commissioner can access and review the results before sharing them with aspirants for verification.
- Aspirants have the opportunity to review their results and provide feedback or raise concerns.
- Once verified, the results are published on the Pax Web App for all students to view.

---

## 4. User Roles

The system supports the following user roles, each with specific permissions and responsibilities:

### 4.1 Aspirants
- **Description:** Students contesting for executive positions.
- **Actions:** Submit nomination forms, view their own results, and provide feedback during the verification phase.

### 4.2 Nominators and Guarantors
- **Description:** Active subgroup members who endorse aspirants.
- **Actions:** Receive email notifications, confirm support via unique links, and provide reasons for their endorsement.

### 4.3 Voters
- **Description:** Registered students eligible to vote.
- **Actions:** Log in to the Pax Web App on the election day to cast their votes.

### 4.4 Electoral Commissioner
- **Description:** Official responsible for overseeing the election process.
- **Actions:** Access and review election results, share results with aspirants for verification, and publish final results on the Pax Web App.

---

## 5. Benefits of the System

The Pax Romana - KNUST Election System offers several advantages over traditional paper-based methods:

### 5.1 Increased Efficiency
- Automates the nomination, verification, and voting processes, reducing the time and effort required for manual handling.
- Provides real-time updates and instant result compilation, minimizing delays in announcing outcomes.

### 5.2 Reduced Paper Usage
- Eliminates the need for physical nomination forms and ballots, contributing to environmental sustainability.
- Lowers administrative costs associated with printing, distributing, and storing paper documents.

### 5.3 Improved Accessibility
- Allows users to participate in the election process from any internet-connected device, enhancing convenience for students.
- Ensures that all registered users can vote securely and anonymously, promoting inclusivity and fairness.

### 5.4 Enhanced Security and Transparency
- Utilizes secure authentication and encryption to protect user data and vote integrity.
- Provides a clear audit trail for nominations, verifications, and votes, ensuring transparency and accountability.
